<h1>AP - Managing a Technical Writing Workflow</h1>

1. Decide on a Technology
	•	XML, Markdown, etc.
	•	Consider compatibility with your tools and platforms
	•	Evaluate ease of use and learning curve for your team
2.	Define Your Data
	•	Metadata/Serialization
	•	How will you parse it?
	•	Establish a data structure or schema
	•	Identify required and optional data fields
3.	Choose a Change Tracking and Approval Workflow
	•	Version control systems (e.g., Git)
	•	Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment (CI/CD)
	•	Define the review and approval process
	•	Determine roles and permissions for team members
4.	Choose Authoring Tools
	•	Text editors or Integrated Development Environments (IDEs)
	•	Collaboration platforms (e.g., Google Docs, Confluence)
	•	Tools for previewing and validating content
	•	Tools for generating final outputs (e.g., PDFs, websites)
5.	Set Up a Documentation Repository
	•	Organize your files and folders
	•	Implement a naming convention
	•	Set up access controls and permissions
	•	Backup and recovery plans
6.	Create Writing Guidelines
	•	Style guide and formatting rules
	•	Templates for consistency
	•	Terminology and glossary management
	•	Accessibility and readability standards
7.	Training and Onboarding
	•	Training materials for new team members
	•	Regular workshops or training sessions
	•	Documentation for using tools and following workflows
	•	Feedback and improvement loop
8.	Monitoring and Maintenance
	•	Regular updates and reviews
	•	Error tracking and correction
	•	User feedback and iterations
	•	Performance metrics and reporting
